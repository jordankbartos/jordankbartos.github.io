Opossum Information

General Information

Common Names: possum, opossum, Virginia opossum
Scientific Name: Didelphis virginiana
Diet: Omnivorous
Size: 2.5 ft.
Weight: 8.8 to 13.2 lbs.

The Virginia opossum is North America's only marsupial. The name opossum comes from the Alongquin name for the animal, "apasum" which means "white animal". Opossums are "living fossils"; they have been around for at least 70 million years. That means they would have co-existed with the dinosaurs! Opossums are known to predate on venemous snakes, and they are highly resistant to snake venom. They are also highly resistent to rabies because their normal body temperatue is uncommonly low - well below the temperature at which the rabies virus's chemistry is designed for. It is exceptionally rare for an opossum to have rabies. Although, they can carry other dangerous diseases such as tuberculosis and leptispirosis.

Physical Properties and Appearance

Opossums have triangular heads and long pointed noses. They are covered in fur everywhere except on their ears, feet, and tails which are bare. Their fur can be white, brown, and/or gray, and thier hairless ears, nose, and tails are pink. In northern climates, they often lose their tails due to frostbite. Opossums have opposable thumb-like fingers that they used to help climb trees and carry things. With 50 teeth, opossums have more teeth than any other North American land mammal.

Behavior

General

Opossums are nocturnal, meaning they are active at night and sleep during the day. They like to find a small space to sleep such as a hole in a tree.

"Playing Opossum"

Opossums are most famous for their habit of playing dead when threatened. The world can be a frightening place for opossums - they are frequently threatened by dogs, cats, foxes, coyotes, bobcats, and humans. When confronted, opossums will usually pass out, foam at the mouth, and emit a foul smell. This is an involuntary reaction, although the opossum is believed to remain fully conscious while in this state. This can last for up to six hours.

Climbing

Opossums are excellent climbers. They have, thumbs, sharp claws, and prehensile tails for gripping tree branches. They spend much of their time up in trees.

Habitat and Range

Opossums can be found in deciduous forests, open woods, and farms. But they prefer wet areas such as marshes, swamps, and streams. They can be found in most of the southern, eastern, and midwestern United States as well as along the west coast and throughout Mexico.

Diet

Opossums are opportunistic omnivores. They graze on grass, nuts, and fruit. They will hunt for mice, birds, ticks, slugs, snails, worms, snakes, and more. An opossum can eat thousands of ticks per week which makes them extremely important in keeping the prevalence of Lyme Disease down. They also scavenge on dead animals which further helps prevent the spread of disease.

Reproduction

The gestation period for an opossum is a very brief 11-13 days. After which, the baby opossums move to the pouch where they continue to develop for the next eight weeks. After that, baby opossums move out of the pouch and ride around on their mother's back for another four to six weeks. Opossums may give birth to 20 babies, but typicaly only up to half of them survive to become independent. Opossums can have up to three litters per year.
